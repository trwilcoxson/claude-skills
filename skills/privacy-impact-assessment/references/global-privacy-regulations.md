# Global Privacy Regulations — Verified Reference

This reference contains verified regulation section/article numbers and descriptions for use by privacy assessment agents. When citing regulations, agents MUST verify citations against this file. If a provision is not listed here, mark it as **[UNVERIFIED]**.

> **Note:** This reference covers key provisions. For complete text, consult official regulation sources.

## United States — CCPA/CPRA (California)

**Full Name:** California Consumer Privacy Act (2018) as amended by California Privacy Rights Act (2020)
**Citation format:** Cal. Civ. Code §1798.[section]

**Applicability Thresholds** (must meet one):
- Annual gross revenue exceeding **$25 million** (adjusted for inflation)
- Annually buys, sells, or shares personal information of **100,000 or more** consumers or households
- Derives **50% or more** of annual revenue from selling or sharing consumers' personal information

| Section | Title | Key Requirement |
|---|---|---|
| §1798.100 | Right to Know | Consumers can request categories and specific pieces of personal information collected in preceding 12 months |
| §1798.100(d) | Disclosure Obligations | Business must disclose categories of PI collected, sources, purposes, and third parties |
| §1798.105 | Right to Delete | Consumers can request deletion of their personal information; business must comply and direct service providers to delete |
| §1798.106 | Right to Correct | Consumers can request correction of inaccurate personal information held by a business |
| §1798.110 | Categories of PI | Right to request: categories of PI collected, categories of sources, business/commercial purpose, categories of third parties, specific pieces of PI |
| §1798.115 | Right to Know PI Sold/Disclosed | Right to know: categories of PI sold or shared, categories of third parties to whom PI was sold/shared, categories of PI disclosed for business purpose |
| §1798.120 | Right to Opt-Out of Sale/Sharing | Right to direct a business not to sell or share personal information; "Do Not Sell or Share My Personal Information" |
| §1798.121 | Right to Limit Use of Sensitive PI | Right to limit a business's use and disclosure of sensitive personal information to specified purposes |
| §1798.125 | Non-Discrimination | Business shall not discriminate against a consumer for exercising any of their rights; no denying goods/services, charging different prices, providing different quality |
| §1798.130 | Methods for Submitting Requests | Requirements for how businesses must handle consumer requests: verification, response timelines (45 days), toll-free number/web form |
| §1798.135 | Do Not Sell or Share Link | Clear and conspicuous link on homepage titled "Do Not Sell or Share My Personal Information"; respect opt-out preference signals |
| §1798.140 | Definitions | Key definitions: personal information (including IP addresses, browsing history, geolocation), consumer, business, service provider, contractor, sale, share, sensitive personal information |
| §1798.145 | Exemptions | Exemptions for: HIPAA-covered entities (for PHI), GLBA-covered entities (for financial data), FCRA activities, certain vehicle data, publicly available information |
| §1798.150 | Private Right of Action | Private right of action for data breaches involving non-encrypted/non-redacted PI; statutory damages $100-$750 per consumer per incident |
| §1798.155 | Administrative Enforcement | California Privacy Protection Agency enforcement; fines up to $2,500 per violation, $7,500 per intentional violation or violation involving minors |
| §1798.185 | Regulations | CPPA authority to adopt regulations, including risk assessment requirements, cybersecurity audit requirements, automated decision-making |
| §1798.199.40 | CPPA Enforcement | California Privacy Protection Agency administrative enforcement authority and procedures |

## Brazil — LGPD (Lei Geral de Protecao de Dados)

**Citation format:** LGPD Art. [number]

| Article | Topic | Key Content |
|---|---|---|
| Art. 1 | Purpose | Protects fundamental rights of liberty, privacy, and personality development of natural persons |
| Art. 5 | Definitions | Personal data, sensitive data, data subject, controller, operator (processor), DPO, ANPD |
| Art. 6 | Processing Principles | 10 principles: purpose, adequacy, necessity, free access, data quality, transparency, security, prevention, non-discrimination, accountability |
| Art. 7 | Legal Bases | 10 legal bases: consent, legal/regulatory obligation, public administration, research, contract performance, exercise of rights, life/physical safety protection, health protection, legitimate interests, credit protection |
| Art. 8 | Consent | Consent must be written (prominent clause) or other means demonstrating subject's will; specific purposes; revocable; separate consent for different purposes |
| Art. 11 | Sensitive Data | Health, genetic, biometric, racial/ethnic origin, religious/philosophical belief, political opinion, trade union membership, sexual life; processing only with specific consent or without consent in enumerated cases |
| Art. 14 | Children's Data | Processing in best interests; specific and prominent consent from at least one parent/legal guardian |
| Art. 15-16 | Termination of Processing | Art. 15: processing ends when purpose achieved, period expired, or subject revokes consent; Art. 16: data elimination after processing ends, except for legal compliance, research, transfer, or legitimate use |
| Art. 17-22 | Data Subject Rights | Art. 17: ownership of personal data; Art. 18: confirmation of processing, access, correction, anonymization/blocking/deletion, portability, information about sharing, revocation of consent; Art. 20: right to review automated decisions; Art. 21: appeal to ANPD; Art. 22: defense of interests before judicial/administrative bodies |
| Art. 33-36 | International Transfers | Permitted when: adequacy decision, appropriate safeguards (SCCs, BCRs, global corporate norms, seals/certifications), specific consent, cooperation agreements; Art. 35: ANPD can approve contractual clauses; Art. 36: international cooperation |

| Art. 41-45 | Sanctions | Art. 41: ANPD administrative sanctions; Art. 42: controller/operator causing damage during processing liable for reparation; Art. 44: processing irregular when not observing legislation or not providing adequate security; Art. 45: indemnification claim rights |
| Art. 46-49 | Security | Art. 46: processing agents must adopt security/technical/administrative measures; Art. 47: agents must observe ANPD standards; Art. 48: controller must communicate to ANPD and data subject security incidents with significant risk; Art. 49: systems structured to meet security requirements |
| Art. 52 | Administrative Penalties | Warning; simple fine up to 2% of revenue in Brazil (limited to R$50 million per infraction); daily fine; publicization of infraction; blocking of personal data; deletion of personal data; suspension/prohibition of processing |
| Art. 55-A thru 55-J | ANPD | Art. 55-A: ANPD created as autonomous body; Art. 55-B: ANPD composition; Art. 55-C: Board of Directors; Art. 55-D: National Council; Art. 55-E: Ombudsman; Art. 55-F: own assets and budget; Art. 55-J: ANPD powers include elaborating guidelines, investigating, applying sanctions |

## Canada — PIPEDA

**Full Name:** Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act
**Citation format:** PIPEDA Principle [number] or PIPEDA §[section]

### Schedule 1 — 10 Fair Information Principles

| Principle | Title | Key Requirement |
|---|---|---|
| Principle 1 | Accountability | Organization responsible for PI under its control; designate individual(s) accountable; protect PI transferred to third parties |
| Principle 2 | Identifying Purposes | Identify purposes for collecting PI before or at time of collection; document purposes |
| Principle 3 | Consent | Knowledge and consent of individual required; form may vary (express, implied, opt-out); can be withdrawn |
| Principle 4 | Limiting Collection | Collection limited to what is necessary for identified purposes; collected by fair and lawful means |
| Principle 5 | Limiting Use, Disclosure, and Retention | PI used or disclosed only for purposes for which collected (or consent/legal requirement); retained only as long as necessary |
| Principle 6 | Accuracy | PI accurate, complete, and up-to-date as necessary for the purposes |
| Principle 7 | Safeguards | Security safeguards appropriate to sensitivity; protect against loss, theft, unauthorized access, disclosure, copying, use, modification |
| Principle 8 | Openness | Policies and practices relating to PI management readily available |
| Principle 9 | Individual Access | Upon request, inform individual of existence, use, and disclosure of their PI; give access; allow challenge of accuracy |
| Principle 10 | Challenging Compliance | Individual able to address challenge concerning compliance to designated individual(s) |

### Key Sections

| Section | Title | Key Requirement |
|---|---|---|
| §5 | Purpose | Purpose of Part 1: establish rules governing collection, use, and disclosure of PI that recognize individuals' right to privacy and organizations' need to collect/use/disclose PI |
| §6.1 | Consent | Validity of consent: reasonable person would consider consent appropriate in circumstances; express consent for sensitive information |
| §7 | Collection Without Consent | Exceptions: journalistic/artistic/literary purpose, federal work/undertaking, publicly available info, collection clearly in interest of individual, legal investigation, national security |
| §8 | Use Without Consent | Exceptions: legal proceedings, threat to life/security, statistical/scholarly research, publicly available info, employment relationship |
| §9 | Disclosure Without Consent | Exceptions: legal proceedings, subpoena/warrant, debt collection, compliance with law, threat to life/security, statistical/scholarly research, national security |
| §10 | Limiting Collection | Organization shall not collect PI indiscriminately; amount and type limited to what necessary |
| §10.1 | Breach Reporting | Mandatory breach reporting to Privacy Commissioner and notification to affected individuals for breaches creating real risk of significant harm |
| §11 | Retention | PI retained only as long as necessary to fulfill purpose; subject to minimum retention obligations under other laws |
| §12 | Access | Individual has right to access PI held about them; 30-day response time; access at minimal or no cost |
| §14 | Complaints | Individual may file complaint with Privacy Commissioner; Commissioner investigates and may pursue court action |

> **Note:** Quebec's Law 25 (An Act to Modernize Legislative Provisions as regards the Protection of Personal Information) imposes additional obligations within Quebec, including mandatory PIAs for certain projects and stricter consent requirements. Federal modernization through Bill C-27 (Consumer Privacy Protection Act / CPPA) is pending.

## China — PIPL (Personal Information Protection Law)

**Citation format:** PIPL Art. [number]

| Article | Topic | Key Content |
|---|---|---|

| Art. 13 | Legal Bases | 7 bases: consent, contract necessity, legal duty, public health emergency, news reporting/public interest, already-public information within reasonable scope, other circumstances prescribed by law |
| Art. 14-16 | Consent | Art. 14: consent must be informed, voluntary, and explicit; separate consent required for specified scenarios; Art. 15: right to withdraw consent (does not affect prior processing); Art. 16: no refusal to provide products/services for refusal of non-essential PI |

| Art. 62-65 | Penalties | Art. 62: non-compliance with law — up to RMB 1M, individuals RMB 10K-100K; Art. 66: serious violations — up to RMB 50M or 5% of previous year's annual turnover; suspension or termination of business; revocation of business license; individuals up to RMB 1M; ban from holding positions |

## Singapore — PDPA

**Full Name:** Personal Data Protection Act 2012 (amended 2020)
**Citation format:** PDPA Part [number] or PDPA §[section]

| Part/Section | Title | Key Requirement |
|---|---|---|
| Part III (§13-17) | Consent Obligation | Consent required for collection, use, or disclosure of personal data; consent may be express or deemed; withdrawal of consent permitted with reasonable notice |
| Part IV (§18) | Purpose Limitation Obligation | Collect, use, or disclose only for purposes that a reasonable person would consider appropriate in the circumstances; purposes notified to individual |
| Part V (§20) | Notification Obligation | Notify individual of purposes for collection, use, or disclosure on or before collection; notification of new purposes before new use |
| Part VI (§21-22) | Access and Correction Obligations | §21: provide access to personal data and information about use/disclosure within preceding year; §22: correct errors or omissions upon request; 30-day response |
| Part VIA (§26H-26J) | Data Portability | Right to request porting of data to another organization in commonly used machine-readable format; applies to data provided by individual and in organization's possession |
| Part VIB (§43-50) | Do Not Call Registry | National registry for individuals to opt out of marketing messages (voice calls, SMS, fax); organizations must check registry before sending marketing messages |
| Part IX (§11) | Data Protection Officer | Every organization must designate at least one DPO; DPO business contact information publicly available |
| Part IXA (§26A-26E) | Data Breach Notification | Mandatory notification to PDPC within **3 calendar days** of assessing breach is notifiable; notify affected individuals as soon as practicable; notifiable = significant harm or significant scale (500+ individuals) |
| Ninth Schedule | Deemed Consent by Notification | Deemed consent where: individual notified of purpose, given reasonable time to opt out, did not opt out; limited to business improvement, operations of organization, and other specified purposes |

**Enforcement:** Personal Data Protection Commission (PDPC)
**Penalties:** Financial penalties up to SGD 1 million or 10% of annual turnover in Singapore (whichever is higher)

## United Kingdom — UK GDPR + Data Protection Act 2018

- Substantially mirrors EU GDPR with UK-specific modifications post-Brexit
- **Citation format:** UK GDPR Art. [same numbering as EU GDPR]; DPA 2018 §[section]
- **Enforcement body:** Information Commissioner's Office (ICO)
- **Maximum fines:** Up to GBP 17.5 million or 4% of total worldwide annual turnover (whichever is higher)

**Key Differences from EU GDPR:**

| Topic | UK Approach | EU GDPR Approach |
|---|---|---|
| Supervisory Authority | ICO (single authority) | National DPAs per member state |
| Adequacy Decisions | UK makes its own adequacy decisions for international transfers | EU adequacy decisions (separate from UK) |
| Age of Consent for Children | **13 years** (DPA 2018 §9) | **16 years** default (member states may lower to 13) |
| Exemptions | DPA 2018 Schedules 2-4 contain UK-specific exemptions (immigration, national security, journalism, research, health/social work, education, regulatory functions) | Member state derogations per GDPR articles |
| Representative | No UK representative requirement for UK-based controllers | Art. 27 representative for non-EU controllers |
| Transfer Mechanisms | UK International Data Transfer Agreement (IDTA), UK Addendum to EU SCCs, UK BCRs | EU SCCs, EU BCRs, adequacy decisions |

**DPA 2018 Key Supplements:**
- Part 2: General processing (supplements UK GDPR)
- Part 3: Law enforcement processing (implements LED)
- Part 4: Intelligence services processing
- Schedule 1: Special categories and criminal data — conditions for processing
- Schedule 2: Exemptions from GDPR provisions
- Schedule 3: Exemptions from law enforcement provisions

## Europe — Swiss revDSG

**Full Name:** Revised Federal Act on Data Protection (revDSG/nLPD)
**Effective:** September 1, 2023
**Citation format:** revDSG Art. [number]
- Similar structure to GDPR but Swiss-specific provisions
- DPIAs required for high-risk processing
- Cross-border transfer protections (adequacy list, SCCs, BCRs)
- Enforcement: Federal Data Protection and Information Commissioner (FDPIC)
- Penalties: fines up to CHF 250,000 against natural persons (not organizations)

## Asia-Pacific — Additional Jurisdictions

### APPI (Japan)

- **Act on the Protection of Personal Information**
- **Citation format:** APPI Art. [number]
- EU adequacy decision in place (mutual)
- Key requirements: purpose specification, limitation of use, proper acquisition, security management, employee/subcontractor oversight, restriction on third-party provision, access/correction/deletion rights
- Enforcement: Personal Information Protection Commission (PPC)
- 2022 amendments strengthened individual rights and introduced mandatory breach notification

### PDPA (Thailand)

- **Personal Data Protection Act B.E. 2562 (2019)**, effective June 1, 2022
- Modeled after GDPR structure
- Key requirements: consent, data subject rights, DPO requirement, DPIA for high-risk processing, cross-border transfer restrictions
- Enforcement: Personal Data Protection Committee (PDPC)
- Penalties: administrative fines up to THB 5 million; criminal penalties for certain offenses

## Middle East / India

### DPDP (India)

- **Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023**
- **Citation format:** DPDP §[section]
- Consent-based processing with clear, specific notice
- Purpose limitation and data minimization
- Reasonable security safeguards
- Data principal rights: access, correction, erasure, grievance redressal, nominate representative
- Significant Data Fiduciaries: additional obligations including DPO appointment, periodic audits, DPIA
- Children's data: verifiable consent of parent/lawful guardian; no tracking/behavioral monitoring/targeted advertising
- Cross-border transfers: to countries not restricted by central government notification
- Penalties: up to INR 250 crore (approx. USD 30 million) per instance
- Enforcement: Data Protection Board of India

### DIFC Data Protection Law (Dubai)

- Dubai International Financial Centre Data Protection Law No. 5 of 2020
- Closely aligned with GDPR
- Enforcement: Commissioner of Data Protection

### ADGM Data Protection Regulations (Abu Dhabi)

- Abu Dhabi Global Market Data Protection Regulations 2021
- Closely aligned with GDPR
- Enforcement: Office of Data Protection
