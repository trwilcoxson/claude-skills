# GDPR Article Reference

## How to Use This Reference

Citation format: "GDPR Art. [number]([paragraph])" — e.g., "GDPR Art. 35(1)"

When citing GDPR articles in the privacy assessment, verify the article number and title exist in this reference. This file contains key articles relevant to privacy impact assessments — it is curated, not exhaustive. If you need to cite an article not listed here, mark it as **[UNVERIFIED]**.

---

## Chapter I — General Provisions (Art. 1-4)

| Article | Title | Key Content |
|---|---|---|
| Art. 1 | Subject matter and objectives | Protects fundamental rights and freedoms of natural persons with regard to personal data processing; ensures free movement of personal data within the EU |
| Art. 2 | Material scope | Applies to processing of personal data wholly or partly by automated means; exceptions for household, law enforcement, common foreign/security policy |
| Art. 3 | Territorial scope | Applies to: (1) controllers/processors established in EU; (2) offering goods/services to EU data subjects; (3) monitoring behavior of EU data subjects |
| Art. 4 | Definitions | Defines key terms: personal data, processing, controller, processor, consent, personal data breach, supervisory authority, etc. |

## Chapter II — Principles (Art. 5-11)

| Article | Title | Key Content |
|---|---|---|
| Art. 5(1)(a) | Lawfulness, fairness, transparency | Personal data must be processed lawfully, fairly, and in a transparent manner |
| Art. 5(1)(b) | Purpose limitation | Collected for specified, explicit, and legitimate purposes; not further processed incompatibly |
| Art. 5(1)(c) | Data minimisation | Adequate, relevant, and limited to what is necessary for the purposes |
| Art. 5(1)(d) | Accuracy | Accurate and, where necessary, kept up to date; inaccurate data erased or rectified without delay |
| Art. 5(1)(e) | Storage limitation | Kept in identifiable form no longer than necessary for the purposes |
| Art. 5(1)(f) | Integrity and confidentiality | Processed with appropriate security, including protection against unauthorized/unlawful processing and accidental loss/destruction/damage |
| Art. 5(2) | Accountability | Controller shall be responsible for, and be able to demonstrate compliance with, the principles |
| Art. 6 | Lawfulness of processing | Six legal bases: (a) consent, (b) contract performance, (c) legal obligation, (d) vital interests, (e) public task, (f) legitimate interests |
| Art. 6(1)(a) | Consent | Data subject has given consent to processing for one or more specific purposes |
| Art. 6(1)(b) | Contract performance | Processing necessary for performance of a contract with the data subject |
| Art. 6(1)(c) | Legal obligation | Processing necessary for compliance with a legal obligation of the controller |
| Art. 6(1)(d) | Vital interests | Processing necessary to protect vital interests of data subject or another natural person |
| Art. 6(1)(e) | Public task | Processing necessary for performance of a task in the public interest |
| Art. 6(1)(f) | Legitimate interests | Processing necessary for legitimate interests of controller or third party, except where overridden by data subject's interests |
| Art. 7 | Conditions for consent | Consent must be freely given, specific, informed, unambiguous; controller must demonstrate consent; withdrawal must be as easy as giving consent |
| Art. 8 | Child's consent (information society services) | Lawful where child is at least 16 years old (member states may lower to 13); below that age, consent by holder of parental responsibility |
| Art. 9 | Special categories of personal data | Processing prohibited for: racial/ethnic origin, political opinions, religious/philosophical beliefs, trade union membership, genetic data, biometric data for identification, health data, sex life/sexual orientation — unless specific exception applies |
| Art. 9(2)(a) | Explicit consent exception | Data subject has given explicit consent (unless EU/member state law provides consent cannot override) |
| Art. 9(2)(b) | Employment/social security exception | Processing necessary for employment, social security, social protection law obligations |
| Art. 9(2)(h) | Health care exception | Processing necessary for medical diagnosis, health/social care treatment, health system management |
| Art. 10 | Criminal conviction data | Processing of personal data relating to criminal convictions only under control of official authority or when authorized by EU/member state law |
| Art. 11 | Processing not requiring identification | Controller not obligated to maintain additional information solely to identify data subject for compliance |

## Chapter III — Rights of the Data Subject (Art. 12-23)

| Article | Title | Key Content |
|---|---|---|
| Art. 12 | Transparent information, communication, modalities | Information in concise, transparent, intelligible, easily accessible form; plain language; respond within 1 month (extendable by 2 months) |
| Art. 13 | Information where data collected from data subject | Must provide: controller identity, DPO contact, purposes, legal basis, recipients, transfer details, retention period, rights, right to withdraw consent, right to lodge complaint, whether provision is statutory/contractual, automated decision-making details |
| Art. 14 | Information where data not obtained from data subject | Same as Art. 13 plus: categories of data, source of data; provide within reasonable period (max 1 month) |
| Art. 15 | Right of access by the data subject | Right to obtain confirmation of processing, access to data, and information about processing; right to obtain a copy |
| Art. 16 | Right to rectification | Right to have inaccurate personal data corrected without undue delay; right to have incomplete data completed |
| Art. 17 | Right to erasure ("right to be forgotten") | Right to deletion when: purpose fulfilled, consent withdrawn, objection with no overriding grounds, unlawful processing, legal obligation, child's data collected for information society services |
| Art. 17(3) | Erasure exceptions | Does not apply where processing necessary for: freedom of expression, legal obligation, public health, archiving/research, legal claims |
| Art. 18 | Right to restriction of processing | Right to restrict processing when: accuracy contested, processing unlawful, controller no longer needs data but subject needs it for legal claims, subject has objected pending verification |
| Art. 19 | Notification obligation | Controller must communicate any rectification, erasure, or restriction to each recipient; inform data subject of recipients on request |
| Art. 20 | Right to data portability | Right to receive personal data in structured, commonly used, machine-readable format; right to transmit to another controller; where technically feasible, right to direct transmission |
| Art. 21 | Right to object | Right to object to processing based on Art. 6(1)(e) or (f), including profiling; controller must stop unless compelling legitimate grounds; right to object to direct marketing at any time |
| Art. 22 | Automated individual decision-making, including profiling | Right not to be subject to decisions based solely on automated processing (including profiling) that produce legal or similarly significant effects; exceptions for contract, EU/member state law, explicit consent; suitable safeguards required |
| Art. 23 | Restrictions | EU or member state law may restrict scope of obligations and rights (Art. 12-22 and Art. 5) for specified purposes (national security, defense, public security, etc.) |

## Chapter IV — Controller and Processor (Art. 24-43)

| Article | Title | Key Content |
|---|---|---|
| Art. 24 | Responsibility of the controller | Implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to ensure and demonstrate compliance; take into account nature, scope, context, purposes, risks |
| Art. 25 | Data protection by design and by default | (1) Implement data protection principles effectively from the outset (pseudonymization, minimization); (2) By default, only personal data necessary for each specific purpose is processed (amount, extent, retention, accessibility) |
| Art. 25(1) | Data protection by design | Implement appropriate technical and organizational measures designed to implement data protection principles effectively |
| Art. 25(2) | Data protection by default | Ensure that by default only personal data which are necessary for each specific purpose are processed |
| Art. 26 | Joint controllers | Where two or more controllers jointly determine purposes and means, they must arrange respective responsibilities by transparent agreement |
| Art. 27 | Representatives of controllers not established in EU | Must designate a representative in the EU (with exceptions) |
| Art. 28 | Processor | Processing only by written contract; processor obligations include: process only on documented instructions, ensure confidentiality, implement security measures, assist with data subject rights, delete/return data after services end, make available audit information |
| Art. 28(3) | Processor contract requirements | Specific mandatory clauses for processor agreements |
| Art. 29 | Processing under authority of controller or processor | Processor and persons under authority must only process on instructions of controller |
| Art. 30 | Records of processing activities (ROPA) | Controller must maintain records containing: name/contact, purposes, categories of subjects/data/recipients, transfers, retention, security measures; processor must maintain records of processing carried out on behalf of each controller |
| Art. 30(5) | ROPA exemption | Not applicable to enterprises with fewer than 250 employees, unless processing likely to result in risk, is not occasional, or includes special categories/criminal data |
| Art. 32 | Security of processing | Implement appropriate technical and organizational measures: (a) pseudonymization and encryption, (b) confidentiality/integrity/availability/resilience, (c) ability to restore, (d) regular testing/assessing/evaluating |
| Art. 33 | Notification of breach to supervisory authority | Notify supervisory authority without undue delay, within 72 hours where feasible, unless unlikely to result in risk; must include: nature, categories/numbers, DPO contact, likely consequences, measures taken |
| Art. 33(1) | 72-hour notification requirement | Without undue delay and, where feasible, not later than 72 hours after becoming aware |
| Art. 34 | Communication of breach to data subject | Communicate to data subject without undue delay when breach likely results in high risk to rights and freedoms; in clear and plain language |
| Art. 35 | Data protection impact assessment (DPIA) | Required when processing likely results in high risk; mandatory for: (a) systematic/extensive evaluation including profiling with legal/significant effects, (b) large-scale processing of Art. 9/10 data, (c) systematic large-scale monitoring of publicly accessible areas |
| Art. 35(1) | DPIA requirement | Where processing likely to result in a high risk to rights and freedoms, controller shall carry out an assessment of the impact of processing operations |
| Art. 35(7) | DPIA content requirements | Must contain: systematic description of processing and purposes, assessment of necessity and proportionality, assessment of risks, measures to address risks |
| Art. 36 | Prior consultation | Consult supervisory authority prior to processing where DPIA indicates processing would result in high risk in absence of measures taken by controller |
| Art. 37 | Designation of DPO | Mandatory for: (a) public authorities, (b) core activities requiring regular and systematic monitoring at large scale, (c) core activities consisting of large-scale processing of Art. 9/10 data |
| Art. 38 | Position of the DPO | Involved properly and in timely manner; provided with resources; no instructions regarding exercise of tasks; reports to highest management level; no conflicts of interest |
| Art. 39 | Tasks of the DPO | Inform and advise; monitor compliance; provide advice on DPIA; cooperate with supervisory authority; act as contact point |
| Art. 40 | Codes of conduct | Associations may prepare codes of conduct for specific sectors; approved by supervisory authority |
| Art. 42 | Certification | Certification mechanisms, seals, and marks for demonstrating compliance; voluntary |
| Art. 43 | Certification bodies | Requirements for accreditation of certification bodies |

## Chapter V — International Transfers (Art. 44-50)

| Article | Title | Key Content |
|---|---|---|
| Art. 44 | General principle for transfers | Transfer to third country or international organization only if conditions of Chapter V are met; all provisions applied to ensure level of protection not undermined |
| Art. 45 | Adequacy decision | Commission may decide third country ensures adequate level of protection; factors: rule of law, supervisory authority, international commitments |
| Art. 45(1) | Transfer based on adequacy | Transfer may take place where Commission has decided the third country ensures an adequate level of protection |
| Art. 46 | Appropriate safeguards | In absence of adequacy decision, transfers with appropriate safeguards: (a) BCRs, (b) SCCs adopted by Commission, (c) SCCs adopted by supervisory authority, (d) approved code of conduct, (e) approved certification |
| Art. 46(2)(c) | Standard contractual clauses (SCCs) | Standard data protection clauses adopted by the Commission |
| Art. 47 | Binding corporate rules (BCRs) | Requirements: legally binding, expressly confer enforceable rights, specify required content (structure, data transfers, safeguards, complaint handling, cooperation with supervisory authorities) |
| Art. 49 | Derogations for specific situations | In absence of adequacy/safeguards: (a) explicit consent after being informed of risks, (b) necessary for contract performance, (c) necessary for important public interest, (d) necessary for legal claims, (e) necessary for vital interests, (f) from public register |
| Art. 49(1)(a) | Explicit consent derogation | Data subject has explicitly consented after being informed of possible risks of such transfers |

## Chapter VI-XI — Enforcement, Remedies, and Penalties

| Article | Title | Key Content |
|---|---|---|
| Art. 51 | Supervisory authority | Each member state provides for one or more independent supervisory authorities |
| Art. 56 | Lead supervisory authority | One-stop-shop mechanism for cross-border processing |
| Art. 58 | Powers of supervisory authorities | Investigative powers (audits, access to data), corrective powers (warnings, orders, bans, fines), advisory powers (opinions, approvals) |
| Art. 77 | Right to lodge complaint with supervisory authority | Every data subject has right to lodge complaint with supervisory authority in their member state of habitual residence, place of work, or place of alleged infringement |
| Art. 79 | Right to effective judicial remedy against controller or processor | Right to effective judicial remedy where data subject considers rights have been infringed |
| Art. 82 | Right to compensation and liability | Any person who has suffered material or non-material damage as a result of an infringement has right to compensation from controller or processor |
| Art. 82(1) | Right to compensation | Right to receive compensation from controller or processor for material or non-material damage |
| Art. 83 | General conditions for imposing administrative fines | Fines must be effective, proportionate, and dissuasive; factors include nature/gravity/duration, intentional/negligent, mitigation, previous infringements, cooperation |
| Art. 83(4) | Administrative fines — lower tier | Up to EUR 10,000,000 or 2% of total worldwide annual turnover (whichever is higher) for infringements of: controller/processor obligations (Art. 8, 11, 25-39, 42, 43), certification body obligations, monitoring body obligations |
| Art. 83(5) | Administrative fines — upper tier | Up to EUR 20,000,000 or 4% of total worldwide annual turnover (whichever is higher) for infringements of: basic principles (Art. 5, 6, 7, 9), data subject rights (Art. 12-22), transfers (Art. 44-49), member state law, non-compliance with supervisory authority orders |
